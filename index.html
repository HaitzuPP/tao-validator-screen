Primary Subnet Validator Screen

9 validators
TAO Institute · Pylon Partners · 2026-07-03
Screen of 9 Bittensor validators ahead of standing up a primary subnet validator. Each is assessed on three questions: what it is for and whether it only validates its own chain, how much of its stake is owner skin-in-the-game versus outside nominators, and whether it charges a staking fee and what that fee has earned. Click any name for the full breakdown.

Key findings

  • 7 of 9 are first-party validators that validate their own subnet (validator coldkey equals the subnet-owner coldkey). All 9 actively validate a single subnet, so none is a general-purpose multi-subnet operator. BitMind and Chutes run as their subnet's primary validator but the owner-coldkey match could not be confirmed.
  • Owner skin-in-the-game varies widely. Self-stake ranges from 0% (Chutes) and 0.08% (Yanez) up to 71% (Nepher) and 54% (Hippius). High self-stake means the operator carries most of the risk; low self-stake means the validator is almost entirely nominator-funded.
  • Three validators charge 0% take (Leadpoet, Hippius, Chutes), earning no commission and passing all emissions to stakers. The rest charge 2% to 18%. The 18% cohort (Nepher, Ditto, Synth, Aurelius) is the standard subnet-owner take.
  • Largest by stake: Chutes (~79,000 TAO, 3,700+ nominators) and BitMind (~14,600 TAO). BitMind has earned the most validator commission at ~1,029 TAO over 504 days.

Data sources: tao.com API vs taostats API

Both APIs were used. They are complementary rather than competing: taostats supplied the on-chain granularity needed to answer the three questions, tao.com supplied cleaner identity and discovery data.

Bottom line. Use taostats for the numbers that matter to a validator decision: commission, self-stake, and reward history. Use tao.com for identity, discovery, APR, and any job where you scan many validators at once and want clean metadata without fighting rate limits. This analysis used taostats for all on-chain figures and tao.com for the identity and purpose layer.
